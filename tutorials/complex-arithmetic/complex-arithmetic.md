## ‚úñÔ∏è Operations with complex numbers

**Two complex numbers $x = a + bi$ and $y=c+di$**

#### **1.Complex addition + -**
$z=x+y= (a+c) + (b+d)i$<br>
$z=x-y= (a-c) + (b-d)i$

#### **2.Complex multiplication x** (Similar to polynomial multiplication)
$z=x.y=(a + bi)(c + di) = ac + adi + bci + bdi^2 = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i$

#### **3.Complex Division**
##### **Complex Conjugate**
The complex number $z=a+bi$, its complex conjugate $w=a-bi$ (Change the sign of the imaginary part)<br/>
So $z.w = (a+bi).(a-bi)=a^2-(bi)^2=a^2+b^2$

Mathematical properties<br/>
$\overline{x.y}=\bar{x}.\bar{y}$<br/>
$\overline{x+y}=\bar{x}+\bar{y}$

To perform division, we multiple the numerator and denominator by Denominator's complex conjugate:

$\frac{x}{y}=\frac{a+bi}{c+di}=\frac{(a+bi)(c-di)}{(c+di)(c-di)}=\frac{(ac+db)+(bc-ad)i}{c^2+d^2}=\frac{ac+db}{c^2+d^2}+ \frac{bc-adi}{c^2+d^2}$
